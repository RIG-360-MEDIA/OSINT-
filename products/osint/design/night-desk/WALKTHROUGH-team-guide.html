1. What is ROBIN-OSINT?

The one-paragraph version, before you click anything.

Think of ROBIN-OSINT as a private newsroom built around one person or organisation — your "principal" (for example, a state government and its leadership). Every day it reads thousands of news stories, social signals, YouTube news clips and official documents, and boils them down into a clear picture: what's happening, who's under pressure, what the mood is, and what to watch next.

It is personalized. The app focuses on the people, places and topics that matter to your brief — so two teammates looking at it may see different stories. That's by design, not a glitch.

What it pulls together

  • News articles (national + regional)
  • Social signals (Reddit, Telegram)
  • Live YouTube news channels & clips
  • Government PDFs & documents

What it gives you

  • A daily written briefing
  • Threat & pressure tracking
  • Charts, quotes and claims
  • A map view and a daily PDF report

3. The six screens, one by one

For each screen: what it's for, what you'll see, and what you can do.

1 Home — “The Briefing” your daily read

What it's for: Your morning brief — the story of the day in plain words.
What you'll see:
  • The Briefing — a short written analysis: what happened, what it means, why it matters, the other side, what's next, and how to play it.
  • Top Stories For You — the most important stories as cards (with picture, headline, mood, source and why it was picked). Ranked by importance, not at random.
  • People To Watch — key figures with their latest headline and a pressure gauge.
  • The Six — six big storylines worth knowing today.
What you can do: Read top-to-bottom, click a story card to open it.

2 War Room — “The Crisis Desk” pressure & threats

What it's for: Seeing who is being attacked or criticised, how hard, and by whom.
What you'll see:
  • Threat Stack — the day's negative storylines as a ranked list, each with a severity badge, a short summary, and who's behind it.
  • The Field — three simple panels: Momentum (how much each figure is being talked about, with the negative share in red), Attack Map (which rivals are pushing which topics), and Alliances (friendly vs hostile outlets).
What you can do: Scan the top of the stack first — that's the loudest pressure right now.

3 Analytics — “The Instrument Panel” the numbers

What it's for: The measured view — charts and counts, no opinion.
What you'll see:
  • The Big Picture — how much coverage there is over time, the top topics, and the overall mood (supportive / neutral / hostile).
  • Who & Where — which people are getting the most attention, and where.
  • The Detail — notable quotes, claims, key numbers, and a wall of real article images.
What you can do: Use it to back up the Briefing with hard figures.

4 Dossier — “Entity Files” deep dive on a person/org

What it's for: A full file on any person or organisation you track.
What you'll see: Search the list on the left, pick a name, and the file opens on the right — their coverage trend, how supportive vs critical the mood is, their share of attention, top topics, their quotes, claims about them, who they're mentioned alongside, English vs Telugu reach, and a timeline.
What you can do: Search by name, filter people vs organisations, and “load more” to read their full story history.
Note — You can only open files for your own principal and watchlist — that keeps everyone's view focused.

5 Map — “The Theatre” the geographic view

What it's for: Seeing where the news is happening on a real map.
What you'll see:
  • A Mine view that zooms into your region, coloured by mood per district — click a district to read its stories.
  • A Global view showing the whole world coloured by how much coverage each country is getting — click a country to drill in.
  • Live layers — wildfire / natural-event hotspots and conflict points.
  • Live Channels — up to six YouTube news streams playing live, below the map.
What you can do: Switch Mine ↔ Global, click any area to drill in, and pan / zoom the map.

6 Dispatch — “The Daily Report” download & email

What it's for: Getting today's full intelligence report as a polished PDF.
What you'll see: A preview of the report, plus two buttons.
What you can do: Download PDF to save a fully formatted report (sections, key numbers, and top stories with pictures), or Email me to send it to your inbox.
Good to know — You also receive this report automatically by email every morning — no clicks needed.

4. Why your screen differs from a colleague's

The single most common question — answered in plain terms.

During setup, you told the app who and what you care about: your principal, the people and organisations on your watchlist, your region, and your topics.

The app then builds your whole picture out of only the stories connected to those choices. So a teammate focused on a different leader, region or set of topics will genuinely see different headlines, charts and maps. There is no single “global feed” — every screen is tuned to your brief.

If your view ever looks wrong, it usually means your setup choices need a tweak — ask an admin to review your preferences.

6. Good to know

A few honest notes so nothing surprises you.

  • The big picture changes slowly. The app summarises over several days, so the headline view can look steady from hour to hour even though new stories are arriving constantly.
  • Some YouTube clips lag. Videos with captions are read instantly; videos without captions can take longer to appear.
  • Twitter / X is hidden for now. It's being collected in the background but isn't shown on screen yet.
  • Everything is read-only and personalized. You can't accidentally change shared data — you're always looking at your own tuned view.
