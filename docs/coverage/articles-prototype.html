Khammam contractor allegations gather steam — fourth outlet picks it up

Allegations of irregularity in the awarding of irrigation-works contracts in Khammam district have now been picked up by a fourth outlet, taking what was a single ground-report into broader political attention. Local agriculture associations have begun escalating to the district collectorate, while the principal opposition party has indicated it will raise the matter in the upcoming session.

The story originated with a single regional outlet on 7 May, drawn from interviews with farmers in three mandals. Since then, two additional Telugu-language outlets and one English-language source have run independent reporting, with corroborating quotes from named witnesses.

Senior officials at the district administration have not formally responded; informal indications suggest a review committee may be constituted. Civil society organisations active in the region have called for an independent audit of the past three years' irrigation contracts.
